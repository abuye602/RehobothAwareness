Mission Work in Ethiopia & Vision for Africa

Partnering with churches and autism advocates to build inclusive, Christ-centered communities where every person can belong and grow.

Our Recent Mission in Ethiopia

We recently had the privilege of serving in Ethiopia as part of a global mission connected to a Southeast Asia prayer network and a broader vision for outreach and discipleship.

During this trip, we partnered with the Nehemiah Autism Center and worked alongside local church leaders to address an often-overlooked need: creating churches that welcome and support individuals with special needs, especially those with autism.

Our team focused on training and equipping leaders with practical tools, biblical understanding, and a clear vision for inclusive ministry. Together, we began developing a framework to help churches become places where every person, regardless of ability, can belong, grow, and participate fully in the life of the church.

Mission Moments

Equip Church Leaders

Provide practical ministry tools and mentorship for pastors, teachers, and volunteers serving families with special needs.

Strengthen Inclusive Ministry

Build church cultures where children and adults with autism are welcomed, supported, and actively included in worship and community life.

Expand the Vision Across Africa

Continue outreach and discipleship by developing partnerships and training models that can be shared with churches across the region.
