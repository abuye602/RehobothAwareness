  • Spiritual Support

    Offering prayer, spiritual guidance, and faith-based counseling to help families cope with the emotional and spiritual aspects of raising a child with autism. This may include support for both the person with autism and their caregivers.

  • Community Building

    Creating a supportive community for individuals with autism and their families, providing opportunities for socialization, connection, and fellowship within a faith setting.

  • Inclusive Worship

    Providing inclusive environments for individuals with autism to participate in religious services, events, and activities. This may involve sensory-friendly services, special accommodations, or programs tailored to the needs of those with ASD.

  • Educational Resources

    Offering resources to help families navigate both the practical and spiritual aspects of autism, such as information on therapy options, IEPs (Individualized Education Plans), or other autism-related topics, all framed through the lens of faith.

  • Advocacy

    Advocating for individuals with autism within the broader religious community, promoting awareness, understanding, and inclusion.

  • Support for Caregivers

    Providing emotional and spiritual support for parents, siblings, and other caregivers, recognizing the unique challenges they face and offering a network of care and encouragement.
